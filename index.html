Stage Fright in an electrifying rock bank known for their high-energy performances and unforgettable sound. Formed in 2020, the band has taken the music world by storm with their unique style and dynamic stage presence.

The Band Members

    Alex Turner

    Lead Vocal

    Alex Turner, the lead vocalist and lyricist of Stage Fright, is a shy and introspective yet charismatic figure. He often retreats into the world of music whenever he needs a means of escape. His first introduction to music was at age 10 when he encountered a live performance in his hometown of Ohio. The raw energy and emotion displayed by the band lit a fire in Alex. His awkwardness grew into quiet rebellion, which made him an ideal frontman for a rock band, though he didn't always feel at ease with the spotlight. His source of inspiration behind his lyrics came from all the feelings of isolation, self-doubt, and pressure he faced during his course of life. He had a distinct, almost haunting voice that could go from fragile and delicate to fiery and intense, which set him apart from other singers in the local scene. Alex frequently battled with insecurity, constantly feeling like an outsider in the music industry. However, this vulnerability became the fuel for his unique vocal style: a mix of delicate falsetto, gritty growls, and passionate delivery that resonated with fans on a personal level.

    Jamie Smith

    Guitarist

    Jamie Smith, the guitarist of Stage Fright, grew up in the bustling city of New York City, a place where the streets hum with the sounds of both struggle and creativity. He had always been drawn to the various sounds of daily life and frequently spent his afternoons sitting on the sidewalks of Lower Manhattan. At 12 years old, he discovered his father's dusty, old acoustic guitar hidden in a closet. The first strum of the guitar let out the most melodic tune Jamie had ever heard. For the next few months, Jamie would save his allowance, and when he had enough, he bought an old electric guitar. Jamie would spend hours on end teaching himself by listening to various albums. He gained a liking to classic rock bands and began replicating various guitar riffs that he liked. He quickly developed an ear for complex melodies and could mimic solos after just a few listens. While he had technical talent, his real gift lay in his ability to make his guitar sing, capturing emotions in his playing that words couldn’t express.

    Taylor Green

    Bassist

    Taylor Green, the bassist of Stage Fright, is the quiet force that anchors the band's sound. Born by the ocean in Boston, Massachusetts, he provides the groove and the grounding with his calm but confident bass sound. His first encounter with a bass guitar was in middle school. A friend introduced him to a local punk band, and during one of their rehearsals, the bassist was absent. Taylor, curious about the role of bass in music, picked up the instrument and began playing along. It was an instant connection. The deep, rhythmic thrum of the bass felt like the pulse of the music, and he was captivated. On his 13th birthday, Taylor was gifted his very own bass guitar, and since then, the bass guitar has never left his side. He used every waking moment learning every bassline he could find, sneaking into clubs to watch bands play, and dreaming of bigger stages. For Taylor, the bass was not just an instrument; it was a way to feel connected to the heartbeat of music itself.

    Morgan Lee

    Drummer

    Morgan Lee, the drummer of Stage Fright, is the rhythmic heartbeat of the band. While neither of his parents had occupations as musicians, they both shared a love for classic rock. As a result of this and the fact that he was born in the music hub of Nashville, Tennessee, Morgan grew up surrounded by music. However, it was his neighbor's drum set that caught Morgan's attention when he was 11 years old. He spent hours visiting his neighbor and hearing him play the drums. At the age of 14, Morgan picked up his first drum kit, a secondhand set he saved up for by mowing lawns and doing odd jobs around his neighborhood. He started teaching himself the basics, banging away on the set in his garage, never bothering with traditional lessons. To him, it wasn’t about perfection; it was about feeling the rhythm, the power, and the release of the drums. By the time he was in high school, Morgan had formed a few bands, always drawn to the energy of live shows and the catharsis of performing. He found his groove in the rock scene, where raw passion trumped perfection.
